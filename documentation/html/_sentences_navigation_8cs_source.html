27  /* APB - Autopilot format B
28  * is sent by some gps receivers to allow them to be used to control an
29  * autopilot unit. This sentence is commonly used by autopilots and contains
30  * navigation receiver warning flag status, cross-track-error, waypoint
31  * arrival status, initial bearing from origin waypoint to the destination,
32  * continuous bearing from present position to destination and recommended
33  * heading-to-steer to destination waypoint for the active navigation leg
34  * of the journey.
35  *
36  * Note: some autopilots, Robertson in particular, misinterpret "bearing from
37  * origin to destination" as "bearing from present position to destination".
38  * This is likely due to the difference between the APB sentence and the APA
39  * sentence. for the APA sentence this would be the correct thing to do for
40  * the data in the same field. APA only differs from APB in this one field
41  * and APA leaves off the last two fields where this distinction is clearly
42  * spelled out. This will result in poor performance if the boat is sufficiently
43  * off-course that the two bearings are different.
44  *
45  * $GPAPB,A,A,0.10,R,N,V,V,011,M,DEST,011,M,011,M*3C
46  *
47  * where:
48  * APB Autopilot format B
49  * A Loran-C blink/SNR warning, general warning
50  * A Loran-C cycle warning
51  * 0.10 cross-track error distance
52  * R steer Right to correct (or L for Left)
53  * N cross-track error units - nautical miles (K for kilometers)
54  * V arrival alarm - circle
55  * V arrival alarm - perpendicular
56  * 011,M magnetic bearing, origin to destination
